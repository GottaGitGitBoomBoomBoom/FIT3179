CO2 Emissions and Renewable Energy in Southeast Asia

This visualization explores CO2 emissions trends and renewable energy developments in Southeast Asia from 2000 to 2020. The focus is on visualizing the changes in emissions across different sectors and countries, as well as the investments made in renewable energy.

The CO2 emissions per capita across the globe, highlighting the largest emitters and trends over the years from 2000 to 2020.

Renewable Energy Capacity vs. Production by Country

Countries like Vietnam and Myanmar exhibit large renewable energy capacity and production, suggesting significant efforts in utilizing renewable resources. Conversely, countries such as Cambodia and Indonesia show considerable renewable capacity but slightly lag behind in production, indicating room for efficiency improvements.

CO2 Emissions by Sector

Southeast Asian countries like Indonesia and Thailand show substantial emissions from both manufacturing and transport sectors, indicating the key contributors to their overall emissions. Countries such as Brunei and Cambodia, with smaller industrial bases, exhibit much lower emissions.

Regional Trends of CO2 Emissions from Manufacturing and Transport

The heatmap shows that countries like Vietnam, Indonesia, and Thailand have consistently high emissions in both manufacturing and transport sectors. Malaysia also has notable emissions, especially from transport. This heatmap effectively highlights sectoral disparities and identifies countries where transport and manufacturing are significant pollution sources.

Renewable Energy Capacity vs GDP per Capita by Group

Middle-income countries like Vietnam demonstrate high renewable energy capacity, signaling their strategic investment in clean energy. In contrast, wealthier nations like Singapore show relatively lower renewable capacity, which may be due to geographic and infrastructural limitations.
